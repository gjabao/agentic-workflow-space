# Carboxy Therapy Competitor Intelligence Report
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Purpose:** Competitive landscape for professional carboxy therapy distributors in North America
**Relevance:** Beauty Connect Shop positioning (KrX Aesthetics distributor, Canadian market)

## Competitor 1: Kin Aesthetics / EsthiSupply
**Website:** https://esthisupply.com
**Brand carried:** KrX Aesthetics (Korean)
**Distributor type:** US-based wholesale distributor for licensed estheticians

### How They Describe Carboxy Therapy
- "An oxygenating treatment that increases circulation, improves skin texture, and has a powerful brightening effect"
- "Works by triggering the Bohr effect — a physiological response that delivers a concentrated surge of oxygen deep into the skin to counteract oxidative stress"
- "Supports essential biological processes such as cell regeneration, angiogenesis, and protein synthesis — all key to resilient, luminous skin"
- Language is science-forward but approachable: Bohr effect explained simply, outcomes-led ("luminous skin," "energized glow")

### Product Line Positioning
- **Both standalone AND add-on:** "May be performed on its own or seamlessly incorporated into facials, hydrofacials, and microneedling services"
- Entry point: Mini Kit (1 CO2 gel tube + 1 mask) — explicitly described as "ideal for estheticians who want to introduce this innovative and highly effective treatment to their clients"
- Mid tier: Full Facial Kit (1 gel tube + 20 facial masks = 20 treatments)
- Body tier: Full Body Kit (1 gel tube + 5 large body masks)
- Beginner-friendly positioning — the mini kit is framed as a low-risk trial to introduce to clients

### Pricing
- Prices are not publicly displayed (requires account/login to view wholesale rates)
- eBay/secondary market data not found for this brand
- Mini Kit implies low buy-in entry product (~1 treatment); Full Facial Kit covers 20 treatments

### Protocol & Training
- **In-person training offered** — includes product overview, selecting the right treatment, pre-care/post-care, contraindications, full protocols, and hands-on treatment practice
- Carboxy is one of ~6 treatments taught in their training (V-Lift, PD-13, Carboxy, Omega Green, Green Sea Peel, One Chemical Peel)
- Written protocols published at: https://esthisupply.com/pages/krx-protocols
- "How to Use Carboxy" blog content: https://esthisupply.com/blogs/news/carboxy

### Target Audience
- Licensed estheticians in the US (wholesale-only)
- No explicit mention of medical spas or dermatologists — purely esthetician-facing language
- Tone is practitioner-education-driven ("bring the latest treatments out of Korea into treatment rooms")

### Key Differentiators
1. **"KrX's largest US distributor"** — exclusivity and brand ownership narrative
2. **Korean skincare positioning** — "bringing the latest treatments out of Korea" differentiates from generic brands
3. **Bohr Effect science** used consistently across all messaging — creates credibility
4. **Ecosystem approach** — carboxy is one treatment in a curated Korean protocol system (KrX, Corthe, Dermathod, Thesera)
5. In-person training ties products to hands-on education
6. Mini Kit entry product lowers the barrier for estheticians to trial

## Competitor 2: Ribeskin / Unlimited Supply Corp (USA) + Dermequip (Canada)
**Official brand site:** https://ribeskin.com
**US distributor:** https://ribeskin-usa.com (Unlimited Supply Corp)
**Canadian distributor:** https://dermequip.com
**Additional US retailer:** https://store.valispa.com
**Brand origin:** Korean (JM BioTech Corporation, developed 2007)

### How They Describe Carboxy Therapy
- "Non-invasive and versatile skin rejuvenating therapy that redefines both facial and full-body care"
- "Needle-free CO2 Carboxy Therapy for professional use"
- "The first non-invasive CO2 CARBOXY THERAPY product for clinics and hospitals in Korea" — provenance and heritage claim
- Science basis: Bohr Effect — "when carbon dioxide from the gel is absorbed by skin cells, hemoglobin in red blood cells releases oxygen, which stimulates blood circulation, metabolism, protein synthesis and disposal of toxins"
- Safety language: "used for 80 years without reported side effects"
- Results language: "skin dramatically brighter, fresher, even-toned, less-lined and supple after first use"

### Product Line Positioning
- Positioned heavily as a **pre/post treatment add-on** to clinical procedures: "perform CO2 Carboxy Therapy right before dermatological procedures to deeply exfoliate pores and allow better absorption of actives"
- Also positioned post-procedure: "after procedures to speed up healing — ideal for post-laser, microneedling, or chemical peel recovery"
- "Enhances the efficacy of dermatological procedures like laser, mesotherapy, peeling and acne extraction"
- Tiered product line:
  - Mini Kit / Mini Combo (5 doses, gel + mask — small trial)
  - Facial 4-Step Combo (CO2 Gel 750ml, 25 facial/neck sheets, Aftercare Soothing Gel, Aftercare Soothing Mask)
  - Facial 6-Step Combo (adds AHA 1% cleanser and Cover Cream)
  - Facial 8-Step Combo Plus (adds retail home care products for resale)
  - Body Professional Combo (3 pieces for body treatments)
- Home care retail products for resale bundled into top-tier kit — revenue model for practitioners

### Pricing
- **4-Step Facial Combo (eBay):** ~$156 USD per unit (bulk pricing at 4+ units)
- **750ml Gel standalone (UK):** £68.73 (~$85–90 USD equivalent)
- Full professional kit pricing gated behind professional account on ribeskin-usa.com and dermequip.com
- Dermequip Canada pricing requires account login (CAD pricing not publicly visible)

### Protocol & Training
- **Ribeskin hosts international seminars** for aesthetic professionals including North America (previously IECSC New York/Be+Well)
- Seminar content: core principles of carboxy therapy, protocol integration, combining CO2 Carboxy with G-Peel and other Ribeskin solutions
- **Vali Academy (USA)** offers Ribeskin certification: sessions hosted by a Certified Ribeskin Trainer for estheticians, students, and spa owners — covers maximizing results, upselling, treatment planning, contraindications
- Protocol cadence published: "Apply MTS once a week and RIBESKIN CO2 Carboxy therapy 2–3 times a week"
- No standalone "certification" product, but clear protocol guides per treatment goal

### Target Audience
- **Broadest target audience of the three competitors:** estheticians, clinic owners, spas, salons, dermatologists, AND plastic surgery clinics
- Explicit messaging: "ideal therapy to be used in dermatological procedures, spas and salons as side treatments"
- "Best choice for maximization of outcomes of dermatological and plastic surgery procedures"
- Medical-adjacent positioning goes further than pure esthetician brands

### Key Differentiators
1. **Clinical heritage claim** — "first non-invasive CO2 carboxy for clinics and hospitals in Korea, developed by JM BioTech 2007"
2. **80 years of carboxytherapy safety data** cited — longest safety record claim in the market
3. **"After first use" results promise** — aggressive outcome claim
4. **Pre AND post-procedure positioning** — most versatile positioning (before laser, after microneedling, etc.)
5. **Body treatment line** explicitly included — not just facial
6. **Home care retail products in top kit** — built-in upsell/retail revenue for practitioners
7. **Medical spa AND esthetician** positioning — broader reach than Korean-focused competitors

## Competitor 3: CLINICCARE USA
**Website:** https://cliniccareusa.com
**Product page:** https://cliniccareusa.com/collections/carboxy-therapy
**Brand origin:** European cosmeceutical brand (not Korean)
**Distributor type:** Direct brand website + authorized distributors (e.g., 4T Medical)

### How They Describe Carboxy Therapy
- "Non-invasive infusion of carbon dioxide gas to increase circulation and stimulate collagen production"
- "Easy, safe and simple treatment which infuses the skin with carbon dioxide to encourage an increased flow of oxygen for facial rejuvenation, resulting in tissue tightening, cell regeneration and an improvement in the appearance of skin"
- Product mechanism: Carboxytherapy Gel applied to face, then Activator Face Mask placed over it — the mask produces CO2 that is infused into the skin
- Benefits claims: reduces acne scars, fine lines and wrinkles, dark under-eye circles; collagen and elastin stimulation; tissue tightening and lightening
- Cosmeceutical brand language: "cutting edge cosmeceuticals," "science meets skincare," "innovative formulas obtained through advanced research, high-quality active ingredients and high concentrations of low molecular weight hyaluronic acid"

### Product Line Positioning
- **Simpler two-component system:** Gel (500ml) + Activator Mask (pack of 20) — no multi-step combo tiers
- European clinical/professional brand positioning — more medical-aesthetic than Korean beauty
- Explicitly "FOR PROFESSIONAL USE ONLY" — gated pricing, login required
- Positioned as a facial rejuvenation service, not explicitly as an add-on enhancer
- Eye area / dark circle indication heavily featured (under-eye treatment angle)

### Pricing
- Professional pricing gated behind account registration — not publicly visible
- No retail/home care component mentioned
- General market context: carboxytherapy treatments retail at ~$75–$200 per session (CareCredit data)

### Protocol & Training
- 4T Medical (distributor) publishes a "CLINICCARE Carboxytherapy Facial Treatment Guide" — step-by-step protocol available at https://www.4tmedical.com/treatment-plan/carboxytherapy-facial-guide/
- No proprietary training certification program found
- Treatment protocol includes clinic sundries (gloves, gauze, mob caps) — packaged for turnkey clinic use
- Application details: 25ml of gel applied evenly, then Activator Mask placed on top to ensure contact

### Target Audience
- **High-end beauty professionals and clinical practitioners** — "rapidly became appreciated by high-end beauty professionals"
- Language suggests medical spas, clinical estheticians, cosmetic clinics
- Not targeting mass-market estheticians — cosmeceutical premium positioning
- UK/EU heritage with US distribution — appeals to clinics wanting European clinical credibility

### Key Differentiators
1. **European cosmeceutical brand** — different provenance story from Korean competitors; appeals to clinics that identify with European clinical aesthetic traditions
2. **Simplest protocol** — two-component system (gel + activator mask) vs. 4–8 step Korean combos — lower learning curve claim
3. **Under-eye/dark circle positioning** — most explicit dark circle/bag treatment angle among the three
4. **"Cutting edge cosmeceuticals"** brand identity — broader skincare line context (CLINICCARE is a full brand, not just carboxy)
5. **Hyaluronic acid integration** messaging — frames carboxy within HA/hydration narrative, not just oxygenation
6. **No home care retail angle** — purely professional clinic product, not retail revenue generator for estheticians

## Implications for Beauty Connect Shop

**Where BCS fits:**
- BCS carries KrX Aesthetics (the same brand as Kin Aesthetics/EsthiSupply in the US) but for the **Canadian market**
- Direct competitor to Dermequip (Ribeskin's Canadian distributor) and any CLINICCARE Canada distributor
- BCS's Korean dermaceutical positioning aligns with Kin Aesthetics' approach but BCS has the clinical/medical spa credibility advantage (KRX + ZENA + Corthe portfolio)

**Messaging gaps BCS can exploit:**
1. **No strong Canadian-specific carboxy distributor** with training — Dermequip is the closest but has no visible training program
2. Competitors all cite Bohr Effect — BCS should own this too but add clinical outcome language (licensed estheticians + medical spas)
3. **Protocol guides + practitioner education** is a differentiator Kin Aesthetics uses well — BCS could replicate this for Canadian practitioners
4. **"Add-on revenue generator"** framing is underused by Canadian distributors — positioning carboxy as a high-margin add-on to existing facial services is a clear email/Klaviyo angle
