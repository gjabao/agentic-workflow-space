## Email 1: Education / Awareness

**Theme:** "The Science Behind the Glow" — Introduce carboxy therapy to practitioners who may not know it, build credibility through clinical evidence, position it as a versatile add-on.

### Subject Line Options

1. `The Bohr Effect: your next treatment room edge` (48 chars)
2. `Why Korean clinics swear by carboxy therapy` (44 chars)
3. `A protocol shift hiding in plain sight` (39 chars)

### Preview Text

- "No device. No needles. No downtime. Just science." (51 chars)
- "How CO2 helps improve skin oxygenation at the cellular level" (60 chars)
- "Clinical-grade results your clients can see in one session" (58 chars)

### Key Education Points

1. **What carboxy therapy is:** A professional-grade CO2 gel-and-mask system that helps improve the appearance of skin through the Bohr Effect — a well-documented physiological response where elevated CO2 encourages hemoglobin to release oxygen to tissues.
2. **Why it matters for practitioners:** No device investment, no needles, learnable in a single training session. It integrates into existing facial protocols as a prep step or standalone treatment.
3. **Clinical credibility (Health Canada compliant):** Published research shows carboxy protocols are associated with improvements in the appearance of dark circles, fine lines, and skin laxity. One peer-reviewed study observed a 47% improvement in dark circle appearance scores. Another randomized trial noted measurable increases in skin density.
4. **Versatility:** Designed for professional use across skin types, including darker skin tones where some device-based alternatives carry higher risk. Summer-safe — no heat, no photosensitivity.
5. **Growing demand:** Already a staple in Korean dermatology and European aesthetic clinics. North American practitioners who adopt early gain a differentiation advantage.

### Recommended Body Structure

**Section 1 — Hook (2-3 sentences)**
Open with a question practitioners can relate to: "What if your most versatile treatment add-on required no device, no consumable tips, and no downtime for your clients?" Keep it benefit-forward. Avoid overselling.

**Section 2 — The Bohr Effect Explained (3-4 sentences + optional simple diagram)**
Explain in accessible, professional language: "When a professional-grade CO2 gel is applied under an activating mask, it raises CO2 concentration at the skin's surface. The body responds through the Bohr Effect — hemoglobin releases more oxygen to the area. This natural process helps improve the appearance of skin tone, firmness, and overall radiance."

Do NOT say "stimulates collagen production" or "repairs skin." Use: "supports the skin's natural renewal process" and "formulated to help improve the appearance of."

**Section 3 — Clinical Evidence Snapshot (bullet list, 3-4 points)**
Present research findings in compliant language:
- "In a published study, participants showed a 47% improvement in dark circle appearance scores over a treatment series"
- "A randomized trial observed measurable increases in skin density parameters"
- "Researchers noted the protocol's favorable safety profile across diverse skin types"
- "Results were maintained for months following the treatment series"

Include a small note: "These findings reflect published research on carboxytherapy protocols. Individual results may vary."

**Section 4 — The Practitioner Advantage (2-3 sentences)**
Frame as a smart business move: "No capital outlay on devices. A 15-20 minute add-on that pairs with your existing facials, hydrofacials, and advanced protocols. One more reason clients rebook."

**Section 5 — CTA**
"Curious how carboxy fits into your treatment menu? We break down the full protocol in our next email."

Secondary CTA: "Explore our carboxy collection" (link to product category page)

### CTA

- Primary: Soft CTA — "Watch for our next email on protocol integration"
- Secondary: "Explore Carboxy Products" button linking to BCS carboxy category page

### Internal Notes for Designer

- **Tone:** Educational, authoritative, warm. This email builds trust. It should feel like a knowledgeable colleague sharing a clinical insight, not a sales pitch.
- **Imagery:** Clean, clinical aesthetic. Consider a simple before/during visual of the gel-and-mask application (no exaggerated before/after). A minimal diagram of the Bohr Effect would add credibility. Use cream (#FAF8F5) background, dark brown (#2C1810) text, gold (#DFBA90) for accent lines or section dividers.
- **Layout:** Short paragraphs. Generous white space. Bullet points for the clinical evidence section. No more than 350 words total body copy.
- **Do NOT include:** Pricing, product-specific details, or hard sales language. This email is pure education.
- **Compliance:** Every benefit claim must use Health Canada safe language. Review copy against the forbidden words list before sending.

### Key Education Points

1. **Protocol simplicity:** Gel application on dry skin, activating mask overlay, 15-20 minutes, remove mask (do not rinse). Can be performed as a standalone or as the first step before extractions, microneedling, hydrofacials, or chemical peels.
2. **Pairing strategies:** Carboxy works as a prep step that helps improve the appearance of skin before advanced treatments. Pairs naturally with Threadfill and HA Filling Powder protocols for enhanced product absorption.
3. **Selling the series to clients:** The transformation happens over 6-8 sessions. Teach practitioners how to frame this: "Think of it like the gym — one session gives you a visible result, but the real transformation happens over 6-8 weeks." Offer series packages at a slight discount to lock in rebookings.
4. **Revenue math:** At $125-250 per session with product cost of $10-17 per treatment, practitioners maintain 60-70%+ margins. A 6-session series at $175/session = $1,050 per client. Monthly maintenance sessions after the initial series create recurring revenue.
5. **Practitioner voices:** Real quotes from professionals using carboxy in their treatment rooms.

### Recommended Body Structure

**Section 1 — Hook (2-3 sentences)**
"You already have the skills. You already have the treatment room. Carboxy therapy fits into your existing workflow in 15 minutes — and gives your clients a reason to come back every two weeks."

**Section 2 — The Protocol, Step by Step (numbered list)**

2. Apply carboxy gel evenly to treatment area
3. Place activating mask over the gel
4. Leave on for 15-20 minutes (clients may notice a gentle fizzing sensation — this is normal and expected)
5. Remove mask. Do not rinse.
6. Continue with your next protocol step (extractions, microneedling, serums) or complete with moisturizer and SPF

## Series-Wide Notes

### Klaviyo Configuration

- **Flow trigger:** Manual campaign send or segment-triggered flow
- **Send cadence:** 3-4 days between each email
- **Conditional splits to consider:**
  - After Email 1: Did they click "Explore Carboxy Products"? If yes, skip to Email 3 (they are ready to buy). If no, continue to Email 2.
  - After Email 2: Did they take the Protocol Quiz? If yes, send a personalized follow-up based on quiz results instead of generic Email 3.
  - After Email 3: Did they purchase? If yes, move to a post-purchase onboarding flow (protocol guide, reorder reminder). If no, wait 7 days and send a single reminder with "Still deciding? Start with the 6-pack."

### Health Canada Compliance Checklist

Before sending any email in this series, verify:

- [ ] No use of "heals," "repairs," "treats," "cures," or "anti-inflammatory" (unqualified)
- [ ] No claims of stimulating specific physiological processes (collagen production, cell regeneration)
- [ ] All benefit claims use approved language: "helps improve the appearance of," "supports skin's natural renewal process," "formulated to help with," "designed for professional use"
- [ ] Clinical study references do not imply guaranteed outcomes
- [ ] "Individual results may vary" disclaimer included where clinical data is cited
- [ ] No before/after images that imply medical-grade transformation
- [ ] Revenue projections include "illustrative" disclaimer

### Approved Language Quick Reference
